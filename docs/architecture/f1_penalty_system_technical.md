# Formula 1 Rules and Penalty System — Technical Reference

*Compiled from the 2025 F1 Sporting Regulations, the FIA International Sporting Code, and the FIA's publicly released 2025 Penalty and Driving Standards Guidelines.*

---

## 1. The regulatory hierarchy

F1 penalties draw from three layered sources:

1. **FIA International Sporting Code (ISC)** — the overarching rulebook for all FIA-sanctioned motorsport. Notably **Appendix B** (Code of Conduct) and **Appendix L** (driving standards on track).
2. **F1 Sporting Regulations** — F1-specific rules. The penalty articles live primarily in **Articles 18, 28, 29, 37, 42, 43, 44, 47, 54, and 58**.
3. **FIA Penalty Guidelines & Driving Standards Guidelines** — *not* part of the rulebook. These are internal stewarding documents made public in **June 2025** to improve transparency. They describe how stewards typically apply penalties, but stewards retain case-by-case discretion.

## 2. Sanctions available to stewards (Article 18 / Article 54.3)

Under **Article 18** of the 2025 Sporting Regulations, stewards may impose any penalty in the regulations *in addition to or instead of* penalties in the ISC. **Article 54.3** is the operative list of in-race / in-session penalties:

| Article | Penalty |
|---|---|
| 54.3 a) | 5-second time penalty |
| 54.3 b) | 10-second time penalty |
| 54.3 c) | Drive-through penalty |
| 54.3 d) | 10-second stop-and-go penalty |
| 54.3 e) | Grid place drop at next event |
| 54.3 f) | Reprimand |
| 54.3 g) | Fine |

Critically, **Article 17.3** prohibits appeals against penalties imposed under 54.3 a)–g), grid drops under Articles 28 and 29, and decisions under Articles 37.4, 42, 43.5, 44.4, and 58.3. A petition for review still requires a **€2,000 deposit**.

## 3. Penalty points (Super Licence)

- Defined in **Article 54.3** of the F1 Sporting Regulations.
- Stewards may apply penalty points whenever a penalty is imposed under the Code or these regulations (excluding standalone warnings/fines).
- Threshold: **12 points within any 12-month rolling window → automatic Super Licence suspension for the next event**. Once served, those 12 points are wiped.
- Points expire individually 12 months after the date of issue.
- Typical issuance: 1 point (minor breach), 2 points (causing a collision, ignoring yellow flags), 3 points (serious collision), 4 points (e.g. breaching a red flag — Lewis Hamilton at the 2025 British GP FP3).

The FIA's published **2025 Penalty and Point Guidelines** (current version: 14 May 2025) note that "the points indicated are intended to be the norm for a particular offence" but stewards may vary them based on **mitigating or aggravating circumstances**, with increases reserved "only in exceptional circumstances."

## 4. The Driving Standards Guidelines (2025 update)

Originally introduced at drivers' request in 2022; substantially revised after a meeting between the FIA F1 Stewards and Drivers at the **2024 Qatar GP**. The 2025 version was published openly in June 2025.

Key principles:

- **Track use (Art. 33.3 of Sporting Regs)**: drivers must make every reasonable effort to use the track and may not leave it without justification. White lines define the track edge — kerbs do *not*.
- **Track limits enforcement**: free practice and qualifying → invalid lap time. Race → "three strikes" before a black-and-white flag, then a penalty (with exceptions for obvious loss of control).
- **Overtaking on the inside**: the overtaking car must be at least alongside (e.g. front axle level with the defending car's mirror) by the apex, must remain controlled, and must be able to make the corner.
- **Overtaking on the outside**: similar conditions; less priority is given to the outside car than in previous versions of the guidelines.
- **Defending on a straight (ISC Appendix L, Ch. IV, Art. 2)**: only **one change of direction** is permitted; returning to the racing line must leave at least one car width to the track edge approaching the corner.
- **Moving under braking** is prohibited.
- **Rejoining the circuit**: must be done safely, without forcing another car off line.
- **Conduct under safety car / VSC / red flag**: penalties for overtaking, speeding, or failing to slow appropriately.

The guidelines list eight factors stewards weigh when judging an incident:

1. How did the cars get to the incident? (late braking, diving in, moving under braking)
2. Was the manoeuvre late or "optimistic"?
3. What could the drivers reasonably see, know, or anticipate?
4. Was the manoeuvre completable on track?
5. Was there understeer / oversteer / locking?
6. Did someone position or handle their car in a way that contributed?
7. Did the corner type contribute? (camber, kerbs, apex geometry)
8. What were the relative tyre compounds, age, and grip?

## 5. Conduct, language, and Appendix B (ISC)

For 2025 the FIA sharpened **Appendix B of the ISC** — the Code of Conduct. Key points from the **2025 Penalty and Point Guidelines (v. 14 May 2025)**:

- **Misconduct, swearing, abusive language**: base fine **€5,000**, scalable up via aggravating factors. Distinguishes between **Controlled Environments** (press conferences, media interviews, podiums) and **Non-Controlled Environments** (in-car radio, paddock recordings). Non-controlled breaches may be treated as private conversations *unless* repeated, racially/discriminatorily abusive, or directed at officials.
- **Public incitement to violence or hatred**: fine **up to €20,000**, with possible escalation under the ISC.
- **Political, religious, or personal statements** in violation of FIA neutrality (without prior written FIA approval): **up to €20,000**, plus possible required public apology.
- **Abuse of officials**: **2025 update (Imola, May 2025)** shifted these from primarily fines to **sporting penalties**, aligning F1 with other major sports.
- First-offence mitigation (within a two-year window) may include suspended penalties or formal warnings — *except* for misconduct toward officials or breach of ISC 12.2.1.n.

## 6. Power unit and gearbox penalties

Each driver has a season-long allocation of power unit components (ICE, MGU-K, MGU-H — note the MGU-H is removed from 2026 — turbocharger, energy store, control electronics). Exceeding the allocation triggers grid penalties:

- First excess of a single component: **10-place grid penalty**.
- Multiple excess components in one race: typically a **pit-lane start**.
- **Gearbox grid penalties were removed for 2025** thanks to improved reliability — gearboxes still must be used for **five consecutive events** (P3, qualifying, race), but no automatic 5-place drop applies.

## 7. Warnings system

Per **Article 18.2** of the Sporting Regulations: a driver who accumulates **5 warnings in a season — at least 4 of which are "driving" warnings** — receives a penalty, typically a **10-place grid drop at the next race**. Driving warnings include things like overtaking in the pit lane, crossing the pit lane entry/exit line, collisions, and running with a damaged car. Warnings reset at season's end.

## 8. Disqualification and exclusion

Disqualification (DSQ) is normally a technical-breach penalty: a car failing post-race scrutineering. Examples:

- **2025 Las Vegas GP**: both McLaren cars disqualified for rearmost skid wear below the **9 mm minimum**.
- Cost cap breaches: can produce fines, constructor point deductions, or testing restrictions.

**Championship exclusion** is the most extreme sanction, reserved for endangering another driver's life or attempting to gain unfair championship advantage — applied once in F1 history (Schumacher, 1997).

## 9. Process: from incident to penalty

1. **Race Director** monitors the session and reports a possible incident to the stewards (Article 47.1).
2. Stewards may also self-initiate an investigation.
3. A notification is sent through the official messaging system within **60 minutes** of session end identifying the driver(s) under investigation; those drivers must remain at the circuit until released (Article 47.2 b).
4. Stewards review video, telemetry, radio, and may summon driver/team representatives.
5. **Standard of fault**: "Unless it is clear to the stewards that a driver was wholly or predominantly to blame for an Incident no penalty will be imposed" (Article 47.2 a).
6. Decision is published. Points (if any) are added to the Super Licence.
7. **Right of Review** (ISC) requires presentation of a **new, significant, and relevant element** unavailable at the time of the original decision.

## 10. Quick-reference offence → typical penalty

| Offence | Typical sanction (norm) |
|---|---|
| Causing a collision (minor) | 5s time penalty + 1–2 penalty points |
| Causing a collision (serious) | 10s time penalty / drive-through + 2–3 penalty points |
| Forcing another driver off track | 5–10s time penalty + 1 penalty point |
| Track limits — race (4th offence) | Black-and-white flag → 5s penalty for further breaches |
| Ignoring yellow flags | 2 penalty points + grid drop or time penalty |
| Overtaking under red flag | 10s stop-and-go + 2 penalty points (e.g. Hamilton, Monaco FP2 2025) |
| Failure to comply with red flag | Up to 4 penalty points + grid penalty (e.g. Hamilton, British GP FP3 2025) |
| Unsafe release | €5,000–€10,000 fine to the team, possible time penalty for driver |
| Speeding in pit lane (race) | Drive-through penalty |
| Speeding in pit lane (practice/qualifying) | Fine |
| More than one change of direction defending | 1 penalty point + warning/time penalty |
| Excess power-unit component | 10-place grid drop or pit-lane start |
| Misconduct/swearing in press conference | Base fine €5,000, scalable |
| Abuse of officials | Sporting penalty (2025 update) |
| Technical non-compliance (post-race scrutineering) | Disqualification |
